So what exactly is ArtSlaw? It is a gallery companion, that gives you a tour of an art exhibition, like a virtual gallery guide. You just past a link and voila, you get the info, you can chat about the exhibition and understand the exhibition. It is my first live webapp, you can try it at [artslaw.io][artslaw].

### My motivation
I often go to art exhibitions and would like to have some background information, to know a little about what I am looking at. Of course one can talk to gallery staff, but sometimes I just want the info quickly, look at it and have an idea about the exhibition, without the interaction. Plus, I don't have much time right now, while parenting almost full time. I have started the Full Stack course last year, but gaining all the knowledge first wouldn't have worked with my schedule. That's why I wnated to learn about vibe coding and see how far I could get on my own.

I think I have gotten pretty far! The webapp has all the parts needed for an MVP:
- Front page
- Login and User Management via Clerk
- Database of your chat history with MongoDB
- A discovery feature to discover new artshows of artists you have researched
- An artblog of current exhibitions

### Where is this project going?
I would like to build on the foundation that I have laid out so far and continue with it. With only a few hours per week, I can still improve the UX and add features. I learn so much about different APIs, not only the Anthropic one. I am also using the knowledge I have gained in the Full Stack course a lot, to give better prompts, understand what Claude Code is doing and where the possibilities and limitations lie.

### Real coding vs vibe coding
I think about the implications of Claude Code and all the other tools a lot. They are not only changing the way we write software, but will also have a great impact on the way we will work in general. I hope the speed of the uptake of these models is still slow, so society has enough time to adjust on the way. And on the positive side I think this whole AI thing could liberate disadvantaged people, that are very motivated to build tools and start businesses, but would be hindered like in the past by not having the right university degree and work experience. 

This dismantling of privilege will, I hope, give rise to a more just society. Naturally, it will unsettle many who have come to believe that years of academic investment entitle them to a particular social standing and a commensurate income. Yet perhaps privilege and the entitlement it breeds ultimately render life stagnant and devoid of genuine challenge. Easy to say, of course, coming from a position of privilege myself — though that tension is a contradiction I frequently sit with, not least when engaging with art.

But enough of this, I hope to grow with artslaw and build a fine product for people to enjoy!
